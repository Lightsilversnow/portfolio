Research other portfolios

Intro

For the research question ‘how can I make the portfolio easy and intuitive to navigate?’ and ‘what should always be easy to find, no matter which page you’re on on the website’ I looked at differ ent example portfolios. With the example portfolios I looked at what information was on a page, how the page is structured, does it have a lot of images, does it have a lot of text and I looked at how the portfolio is navigated. I don’t want to divert too much from existing portfolios, because people can get overwhelmed by information that might be on a page and they can get lost if the navigation is something very different than what they are used to.

Conclusion

For the research question what should always be easy to find, no matter which page you’re on on the website, the navigation bar should show when you scroll up. The navbar should include a link to the home page, your projects and what’s important for your website, for instance the learning outcomes.

For the research question how can I make the portfolio easy and intuitive to navigate it’s important to not divert too much from existing examples. This makes it easier for people to find everything on the page and makes it less frustrating to use. The homepage should be a brief overview of the project highlights. The project page should show the projects with mostly images and a very brief description. The projects themselves should show the project and use images as much as possible instead of describing the project.
